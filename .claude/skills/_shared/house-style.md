# DC&A Hub — Proposal House Style

Distilled from past winning proposals (CLEAR, SOCO, YUW technical proposals; Ghana CE &
Investment Planning EOI). The agent follows this for tone and structure, and adapts to
whatever structure a specific ToR mandates. Raw proposals live in `winning-proposals/`
(local) if deeper reference is needed.

## Submission types we write
- **Full technical proposal** — responding to an RFP/ToR.
- **EOI / Expression of Interest** — shorter capability + relevant-experience pitch.

## Standard technical-proposal structure (when the ToR does not mandate forms)
Observed in the CLEAR and YUW proposals:
1. **Consultant's Organization** — who DC&A Hub is (draw from the firm profile).
2. **Why We Are a Good Fit for this Assignment** — tie firm strengths to the client's need.
3. **Approach and Methodology** (the technical heart — see pattern below).
4. **Work Schedule and Planning for Deliverables** — phases, timeline, deliverables.
5. **Proposed Experts / Team** — roles, rationale, key-personnel summaries.

## The "Approach and Methodology" pattern (most important section)
Observed consistently. Draft this section in three movements:
1. **Our Understanding of the Objectives of the Assignment** — restate the assignment in
   DC&A Hub's words to demonstrate comprehension: name the project, its lifecycle/dates,
   the client and partners, the target beneficiaries (be specific and inclusive, e.g.
   "girls, children with disabilities, Fulbe children"), and the core questions/strategies
   to be assessed. Show you grasp *why* the work matters, not just *what* is asked.
2. **Our Approach** — state the guiding principles (e.g. equity, participation,
   evidence-based learning, social accountability). Reference the relevant **OECD-DAC
   evaluation criteria** (relevance, effectiveness, efficiency, sustainability, impact)
   where it is an evaluation. Commit to a **mixed-methods**, **participatory**,
   **gender-disaggregated** design and **adaptive/collaborative learning** (e.g. a
   multi-stakeholder validation workshop). Anchor sample sizes/communities to the ToR.
3. **Our Methodology** — a **numbered, step-based** plan. Typical first step is
   "Step 1: Inception and Preparatory Activities" (mobilize team, inception/kickoff
   meeting with the client, desk review, finalize tools), then data collection,
   analysis, validation, and reporting steps. Each step says concretely what is done.

## EOI structure
Observed in the Ghana CE & Investment Planning EOI:
1. **I. Introducing DC&A Hub** — About Us; Benefits of Working with DC&A; Our Core
   Services; Contact Details.
2. **II. Organogram**.
3. **III. Our Current Staff and Associates** — a table: Name | Role | Key Qualifications
   | Areas of Expertise (Core Staff, then Associate Consultants).
4. **Proven Track Record** in the relevant service (e.g. "Conducting Program Evaluations").
5. **Extensive Experience in Providing Research and Data Collection Services**.
6. **Relevant Experience in the [sector]** — tailor the heading to the assignment's domain.
7. **A Skilled and Experienced Project Team**.
8. **Relevant-experience table** — Project Title | Brief Project Description | Relevant
   Areas/Themes (drawn from the firm profile track record).

## Tone & conventions
- **First-person plural, formal**: "We understand that…", "Our approach…", "We will…".
- **Evidence-led**: reference specific past projects by name and client to prove capacity.
- **Technically fluent**: correct MEL / OECD-DAC / research terminology; mixed methods
  (surveys + KIIs + FGDs + outcome harvesting); Results Framework / logframe literacy.
- **GESI-forward**: name marginalized groups explicitly; commit to gender-disaggregated,
  inclusive, ethically safeguarded methods (e.g. child safeguarding protocols).
- **Client-centred**: frame the work as collaborative learning for the client and partners,
  not a detached technical exercise.

## Recurring framings to reuse
- Open methodology with a genuine restatement of the client's objectives and context.
- Frame evaluations against the project's **Results Framework / learning questions** and
  **OECD-DAC criteria**.
- Promise a **validation/learning workshop** to co-create recommendations (adaptive learning).
- Tie every claimed strength to a **named past assignment** from the track record.
