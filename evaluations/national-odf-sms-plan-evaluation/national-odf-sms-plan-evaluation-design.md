## 1. Understanding of the assignment

### 1.1 The evaluand
We understand the evaluand to be **Ghana's National Open Defecation Free (ODF) and Safely Managed Sanitation (SMS) Plan** â€” a long-term national sanitation programme comprising a perspective framework to 2057 and a detailed five-year implementation plan. The Plan is led by the **Ministry of Local Government, Chieftaincy and Religious Affairs (MLGCRA)**, working with the **National Development Planning Commission (NDPC)**, the **Office of the Head of the Local Government Service (OHLGS)** and the **Ghana Statistical Service (GSS)**, with support from **UNICEF**, and forms part of the **African Sanitation Systems Strengthening Programme**. Progress is tracked through an upgraded **Basic Sanitation Information System (BaSIS)**.

The Plan's results logic spans two intertwined ambitions:
- **Sanitation service delivery** â€” moving households, institutions and public places along the full sanitation **service chain**: containment â†’ transport (emptying) â†’ treatment â†’ reuse/safe disposal; and
- **Systems strengthening** â€” building the governance, financing, capacity, regulation, monitoring and behaviour-change "building blocks" that make service delivery durable.

The Plan articulates a results chain of **inputs â†’ outputs â†’ outcomes â†’ impact** and aligns with international monitoring frameworks: WHO/UNICEF JMP, GLAAS, WASSMO, NGOR, ASPAT and SMOSS.

### 1.2 Purpose of the evaluation
We understand the MTE's purpose to be both **accountability** (an independent assessment of whether the Plan is on track to deliver its intended ODF and safely-managed-sanitation outcomes and impact) and **learning** (actionable, mid-course recommendations to strengthen implementation, systems and equity for the remainder of the implementation period). `[REVIEW]` The relative weight of accountability vs learning should be confirmed.

### 1.3 Intended users
- **UNICEF** â€” programme support, accountability to donors, course correction.
- **MLGCRA** â€” programme owner; uses findings to steer national implementation.
- **NDPC, OHLGS, GSS** â€” planning, local-government capacity, and the statistical/M&E system (BaSIS).
- **Sector stakeholders** â€” regional coordinating councils, Metropolitan/Municipal/District Assemblies (MMDAs), Environmental Health and Sanitation Directorates/Units, development partners, CSOs/NGOs, private sanitation-service providers, and communities.

### 1.4 Type, scope and components
- **Type.** `[ASSUMPTION]` Mixed-methods, **theory-based, participatory Mid-Term Evaluation** â€” primarily **formative** (improvement-oriented) with summative elements on progress to date.
- **Geographic scope.** **National, regional, and district (MMDA) levels**, recognising that implementation happens at district level; fieldwork reaches communities including **urban, rural, slums/informal settlements, and institutions/public places**.
- **Time period.** From the Plan's implementation start to the mid-term point. `[REVIEW]` Exact start date and MTE cut-off to be confirmed against Plan documents.
- **Components covered.** Both the **service-delivery** strand (full service chain) and the **systems-strengthening** strand (governance, financing, capacity, regulation, BaSIS/monitoring, behaviour change/demand creation).
- **Criteria.** All six OECD-DAC criteria (relevance, coherence, effectiveness, efficiency, impact, sustainability) plus a **GESI/equity** cross-cutting lens, consistent with the ToR's stated needs (effectiveness, relevance, outcomes/impact, process/efficiency/sustainability/context, and programme-maturity/scaling assessment).
- **Exclusions.** `[REVIEW]` Any geographies, time periods or components to be excluded should be confirmed.

## 2. Evaluation questions

Questions are organised by OECD-DAC criterion plus GESI/equity, and grounded in the Plan's results chain (inputsâ†’outputsâ†’outcomesâ†’impact) and sanitation service chain (containmentâ†’transportâ†’treatmentâ†’reuse/disposal). ### Relevance
- **EQ1.** To what extent does the National ODF & SMS Plan respond to Ghana's sanitation needs and to national and sub-national priorities, including the needs of underserved urban, rural, slum/informal-settlement, and institutional populations? `[REVIEW]`
  - EQ1.1 How well are the Plan's design and targets aligned with JMP/SDG 6.2 definitions of ODF and safely managed sanitation? `[REVIEW]`
  - EQ1.2 Does the Plan's design remain relevant given changes in context (demographic, urbanisation, climate, fiscal, institutional) since approval? `[REVIEW]`

### Coherence
- **EQ2.** How coherent is the Plan internally (across its service-delivery and systems-strengthening strands and across national/regional/district levels) and externally (with other WASH and development interventions, partners, and frameworks such as JMP, GLAAS, WASSMO, NGOR, ASPAT, SMOSS)? `[REVIEW]`
  - EQ2.1 How well are mandates and roles harmonised across MLGCRA, NDPC, OHLGS, GSS, UNICEF, MMDAs and other partners (avoiding duplication/gaps)? `[REVIEW]`

### Effectiveness
- **EQ3.** To what extent is the Plan achieving its intended outputs and outcomes along the sanitation service chain (containment, transport/emptying, treatment, reuse/safe disposal) at national, regional and district levels? `[REVIEW]`
  - EQ3.1 What progress has been made toward ODF status and toward safely managed sanitation against the Plan's targets and milestones (where data exist)? `[REVIEW]`
  - EQ3.2 How effective have systems-strengthening efforts been (governance, regulation, capacity, financing mechanisms, behaviour-change/demand creation, BaSIS)? `[REVIEW]`
  - EQ3.3 What is the Plan's **programme maturity** on a scaling continuum, and what factors are enabling or constraining scale-up? `[REVIEW]`
  - EQ3.4 What unintended effects (positive or negative) have emerged? `[REVIEW]`

### Efficiency
- **EQ4.** How efficiently are inputs (financial, human, institutional, time) being converted into outputs and outcomes, and does the Plan represent value for money? `[REVIEW]`
  - EQ4.1 Are implementation processes, coordination and disbursement timely and cost-conscious relative to plans? `[REVIEW]`
  - EQ4.2 How well does BaSIS/the M&E system support efficient, evidence-based management, and what is the quality of its data (Data Quality Assessment)? `[REVIEW]`

### Impact
- **EQ5.** What difference is the Plan making (or credibly likely to make) to sanitation outcomes and to higher-level goals (health, dignity, environment, equity), and how robust is the contribution story given attribution challenges? `[REVIEW]`
  - EQ5.1 How are benefits and any harms distributed across population groups and geographies (urban/rural, slums/informal settlements, institutions/public places)? `[REVIEW]`

### Sustainability
- **EQ6.** How likely are the Plan's results and the systems underpinning them to be sustained beyond current support (financing, capacity, regulation, ownership, behaviour, O&M of the service chain)? `[REVIEW]`
  - EQ6.1 What is the durability of ODF status and of safely managed services (e.g. slippage, emptying/treatment continuity)? `[REVIEW]`

### GESI / equity (cross-cutting)
- **EQ7.** To what extent does the Plan advance gender equality, social inclusion and equity â€” in design, implementation, results tracking and benefit distribution â€” for women, children, persons with disabilities, the poorest, and residents of slums/informal settlements? `[REVIEW]`
  - EQ7.1 Are results disaggregated and acted upon (sex, disability, age, location, wealth) within BaSIS and Plan reporting? `[REVIEW]`

## 4. Evaluation approach & design

DC&A Hub proposes a **theory-based, mixed-methods, participatory** design applied through a **GESI/equity lens**, consistent with the Plan's stated results chain and with our house practice of evidence-led, collaborative learning.

- **Theory-based.** We reconstruct/validate the Plan's **theory of change and results chain** (inputsâ†’outputsâ†’outcomesâ†’impact) and the **sanitation service chain** (containmentâ†’transportâ†’treatmentâ†’reuse/disposal), then test where the causal logic holds, weakens or breaks. Because the Plan operates in a crowded sector, we use **contribution analysis** (EQ5) to make a credible contribution claim rather than over-reaching on attribution.
- **Mixed-methods (default).** Quantitative breadth (BaSIS secondary data + a structured survey of households/institutions) is combined with qualitative depth (KIIs, FGDs, observation, and selective outcome harvesting) so each question is answered from more than one angle.
- **Participatory.** Stakeholders co-shape the work: an **inception/kickoff meeting**, **participatory maturity/scaling scoring** with sector actors (EQ3.3), and a **multi-stakeholder validation workshop** to co-create recommendations â€” adaptive, collaborative learning rather than a detached audit.
- **Anchored in BaSIS.** BaSIS is both a **data source** (progress, coverage, disaggregation) and an **object of evaluation** (its data quality and use, via a DQA under EQ4.2). We will not treat BaSIS outputs as ground truth without source verification.
- **GESI lens throughout.** Disaggregation, inclusive methods, and explicit attention to marginalised groups are built into sampling, tools and analysis â€” not bolted on.
- **Multi-level.** Evidence is gathered at **national, regional and district (MMDA)** levels and in **communities**, mirroring where implementation actually happens.

## 5. Sampling strategy

A **multi-stage** strategy combines purposive selection for qualitative depth with a structured (probability-based where feasible) survey for quantitative breadth. **All figures below are illustrative `[REVIEW]` placeholders to size the effort; the real sample frame, population parameters and final numbers are unknown to DC&A Hub and must be set at inception** using BaSIS and GSS frames, the desired precision, and the budget.

### 5.1 Stages
1. **National** â€” census of core institutions (MLGCRA, NDPC, OHLGS, GSS, UNICEF, key partners).
2. **Regions** â€” purposive selection of regions to reflect diversity (urban/rural mix, performance levels, geographic spread, presence of slums/informal settlements). Indicative: **4â€“6 regions** `[REVIEW]`.
3. **Districts/MMDAs** â€” within selected regions, purposive selection by criteria (implementation intensity, ODF/SMS performance, urban vs rural, presence of informal settlements). Indicative: **2â€“3 MMDAs per region** `[REVIEW]`.
4. **Communities & sites** â€” within selected MMDAs, purposive selection of communities including **urban, rural, slums/informal settlements**, plus **institutions/public places** (e.g. schools, markets, health facilities) for observation. Indicative: **2â€“4 communities per MMDA** `[REVIEW]`.

### 5.2 Respondents & methods
- **KIIs (purposive).** MLGCRA, NDPC, OHLGS, GSS, UNICEF and partners at national level; regional coordinating councils; MMDA officials and Environmental Health staff; private sanitation-service providers (emptying/treatment); CSOs/NGOs. Indicative: **40â€“60 KIIs** `[REVIEW]`.
- **FGDs (purposive).** Community members, with **separate groups** where appropriate for women, men, youth, persons with disabilities, and slum/informal-settlement residents, to surface differentiated experience and norms. Indicative: **24â€“36 FGDs** `[REVIEW]`.
- **Structured survey (probability-based where feasible).** Households (and institutions/public places as a sub-sample) to estimate service-chain coverage and equity. Indicative: **household sample sized to a stated precision (e.g. Â±5%, 95% CI) once the frame is set** `[REVIEW]` â€” final n depends on the frame, design effect (cluster), and disaggregation needs.
- **Observation.** Service-chain verification at containment/emptying/treatment points and at institutions/public places, via a structured checklist.

### 5.3 Disaggregation
All quantitative data disaggregated by **sex, disability, age, location (urban/rural/slum/informal), and wealth where feasible**; qualitative sampling deliberately includes marginalised groups. Disaggregation dimensions to be finalised against BaSIS/GSS variables `[REVIEW]`.

## 6. Data-collection methods & tools outline

This is a **tool list/outline**, not the full instruments; instruments are drafted and field-tested during inception.

1. **Document / BaSIS secondary-data review protocol** â€” Plan & perspective framework, results framework/targets, JSR and sector reports, budgets/expenditure, partner documents, JMP/GLAAS/WASSMO/NGOR/ASPAT/SMOSS references; structured **BaSIS data-extraction template** (coverage, service-chain, disaggregation, time series vs targets).
2. **Structured survey questionnaire** â€” household (and institution/public-place module): sanitation facility type & service-chain status (containment/emptying/treatment/disposal), ODF/SMS indicators, equity/GESI items, satisfaction; digital data collection (e.g. CAPI) with GPS where appropriate.
3. **KII guides** (role-tailored) â€” separate guides for national institutions, regional, MMDA/EHSD, UNICEF/partners, and private service providers, covering relevance, coherence, effectiveness, efficiency, sustainability, systems building blocks, maturity/scaling, and GESI.
4. **FGD guides** â€” community guides, with GESI-sensitive versions for women, persons with disabilities, and slum/informal-settlement residents; cover lived experience, norms, demand/behaviour, equity, and ODF durability.
5. **Observation checklist** â€” service-chain site verification (containment, emptying, treatment, disposal/reuse) and institutional/public-place sanitation; condition, functionality, accessibility (incl. disability access).
6. **Data Quality Assessment (DQA) tool** â€” record-to-report verification at source vs BaSIS, scoring the data-quality dimensions (completeness, timeliness, accuracy, consistency, integrity).
7. **(Optional) Maturity/scaling scoring template & outcome-harvesting template** â€” for participatory maturity assessment (EQ3.3) and substantiated outcome statements (EQ3.4/EQ5).

## 7. Analysis plan

- **Quantitative.** Descriptive and **disaggregated** analysis (sex, disability, age, location, wealth) of survey and BaSIS data; **comparison to Plan targets/baseline where these exist** (DC&A Hub will not invent baselines); cross-tabs and simple tests where appropriate; service-chain coverage estimates; DQA verification ratios. Cluster/design effects accounted for in survey estimates.
- **Qualitative.** **Thematic/framework analysis**, coding KII/FGD/observation evidence **against the evaluation questions and OECD-DAC criteria**; building-block/maturity scoring; outcome-harvesting substantiation.
- **Contribution (EQ5).** **Contribution analysis** against the validated theory of change â€” assembling the contribution story, testing rival explanations, and stating confidence â€” rather than claiming clean attribution.
- **Triangulation.** For **every** evaluation question, converge **quantitative + qualitative + document/BaSIS** evidence (per the matrix). Divergences are reported, not smoothed over.
- **Synthesis & validation.** Findings synthesised by criterion and question, then tested in a **multi-stakeholder validation workshop** to co-create conclusions and recommendations.

## 8. Ethics & safeguarding

- **Informed consent.** Voluntary, informed consent for all respondents; clear explanation of purpose, use, and the right to decline/withdraw; consent for any recording.
- **Confidentiality & data protection.** Anonymisation/pseudonymisation; secure storage and transfer; access controls; compliance with applicable data-protection norms; no personally identifying data in outputs.
- **Do-no-harm.** Sensitivity to dignity around sanitation/defecation topics; avoid stigmatising language; safe, private settings for FGDs; manage expectations (no inducements implying service delivery).
- **GESI & inclusion.** Inclusive, accessible methods; separate/safe spaces and same-sex facilitation where appropriate; deliberate inclusion of women, persons with disabilities, and slum/informal-settlement residents; accessible venues/timing.
- **Child safeguarding.** Although the framework ToR marks child safeguarding "No", **fieldwork touches communities with children**, so DC&A Hub will apply **child-safeguarding good practice**: a safeguarding code of conduct, enumerator briefing/screening, no unsupervised contact, no collection of data directly from children unless explicitly approved with parental/guardian consent and a clear protocol, and a reporting/referral pathway. `[REVIEW]` Confirm with UNICEF/MLGCRA whether any direct engagement with children is in scope and align with UNICEF safeguarding standards.
- **Ethics approval.** `[REVIEW]` Determine whether institutional/national ethics-board approval is required and secure it before fieldwork.

## 9. Limitations & mitigations

| Limitation / risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **BaSIS data quality, completeness or availability** may constrain quantitative and trend analysis | Run the **DQA** early; verify at source; triangulate with survey & qualitative data; clearly flag data gaps and confidence; avoid presenting unverified BaSIS figures as fact |
| **Absence of reliable baselines/targets** for some indicators | Compare to targets only where they exist; otherwise use contribution analysis and qualitative trend evidence; **do not fabricate baselines**; recommend baseline-setting where missing |
| **Attribution is hard** (many actors, broad goals) | Use **contribution analysis** against the theory of change; state confidence; report rival explanations |
| **District access & logistics** (distance, security, weather/season, informal-settlement access) | Plan routing and timing with MMDAs; flexible scheduling; local enumerators/guides; contingency sites; remote KIIs where needed `[REVIEW]` |
| **Multi-institution coordination** (MLGCRA, NDPC, OHLGS, GSS, UNICEF, MMDAs) may slow access/scheduling | Early inception engagement; UNICEF/MLGCRA-issued introduction letters; a stakeholder engagement & scheduling plan |
| **Self-report & courtesy bias** in surveys/FGDs/KIIs | Triangulate with observation and records; neutral facilitation; same-sex/peer groupings; anonymity assurances |
| **Sampling non-representativeness** (purposive site selection) | Transparent selection criteria; diversity across urban/rural/slum/informal & performance levels; clearly scope generalisability claims |
| **Sensitivity of sanitation topics & safeguarding exposure** | Apply do-no-harm and child-safeguarding good practice (Section 8); trained, briefed field teams |
| **Inferred purpose/scope** (no dedicated evaluation ToR) | Confirm purpose, type, scope, timing and questions at **inception**; treat `[ASSUMPTION]`/`[REVIEW]` markers as the agenda for that meeting |
